FAQ
What is USDC and why does it matter for player props?
USDC is a stablecoin — always worth exactly $1 USD, issued by Circle and backed by cash reserves. It lets crypto-native platforms pay out instantly without bank delays, while avoiding the price volatility of Bitcoin or Ethereum.
Is it legal to play player props with crypto in the US?
Player props in a DFS-style contest format are legal in the majority of US states. Kalshi, as a CFTC-regulated exchange, is also US-legal. The payment method — crypto vs card — doesn't change the legal classification of the game itself.
What's the difference between player props and prediction markets?
Player props ask you to predict whether a player will go over or under a specific stat line (e.g., Mahomes over 280 passing yards). Prediction markets like Polymarket and Kalshi include player props but also broader market questions. The formats overlap significantly at the player level.
How do I get USDC to start playing?
You can buy USDC on any major exchange — Coinbase, Kraken, Binance, or others. Transfer it to a non-custodial wallet like Coinbase Wallet or MetaMask, then connect to whichever platform you want to use.
